Conclusion

To conclude, the Taj Mahal is a wonderful work of architecture; it is literally one of the best ever created, yet it was made almost 400 years ago. It is highly evident that master craftsman designed and worked on this project, and the physical beauty is also quite obvious, but there is a much subtler beauty that puts this work over the edge and makes it one of the greats, and that is the geometry that is involved within the work. The whole complex is symmetrical, which is not very common in a work of this size, and it contains many tessellations and fractals that subtly unite the work as a whole. This all comes together in a way that greatly appeals to humans because of our often unconscious affinity for even, perfect geometric representations which the creators of the Taj Mahal were amazingly able to implement during the mid 1600s.
